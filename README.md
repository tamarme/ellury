# Ellury

**Ellury** is a name drawn from multiple layers of meaning — the Georgian words *ველური* (wild) and *ელვარე* (radiant), and a word that feels deeply feminine, intuitively linked to the unconscious.

---

## What is this?

**Ellury** is an experiment in tracking neural impulses that originate below conscious awareness.

The neocortex is exceptional at logical reasoning, but it often struggles to accurately interpret signals from the unconscious — desires, needs, and states that haven't yet surfaced into clear thought. ellury is an attempt to make those signals visible.

The core question this project is trying to answer:

> *Is there a repeatable, detectable signal that comes from the unconscious — and can we read it?*

---

## How it works (current stage)

The initial approach uses sensors already present in a mobile phone:

- **Accelerometer** — measures movement and physical micro-responses
- **Gyroscope** — captures orientation shifts and subtle rotational changes
- Other available motion and environmental sensors

The hypothesis is that unconscious impulses may manifest as measurable, repeating physical patterns — even ones we are not consciously aware of producing. This stage is purely observational: the goal is to detect whether any consistent signal exists at all before drawing conclusions.

---

## Stage

This is early-stage experimental research. There is no product yet — only a question and a method being tested. The first milestone is simply to observe whether repeating, unconscious-origin signals can be detected at all.

---

## Who is it for?

The primary focus is **self-help** — giving individuals a tool to better understand their own unconscious desires and needs. Clinical applications may become possible later, but that is not the current scope.
